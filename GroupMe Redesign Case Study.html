Redesigning the User Interface of GroupMe Mobile Application – a UX Case Study

Kyle Wedemeyer

Introduction

GroupMe is a mobile group messaging app owned by Microsoft available on both mobile devices and desktop. GroupMe is widely used among students in colleges and universities to facilitate communication for classes, clubs and organizations. In many ways GroupMe has provided an alternative form of communication as compared to email or SMS. As a central form of communication for college students it is important that the user interface is simple and clear to navigate.

App Analysis

GroupMe is a useful app to contact large amounts of people in a more casual manner when compared to email. However, it is still somewhat disorganized and occasionally glitchy. It is an incredibly useful tool but still feels unpolished in some areas that could use minor touch-ups to majorly improve the overall user experience. The organization of all the Direct Messages and Group Chats and mass amount of messages that a user receives can be overwhelming.

User Research

Survey respondents filled a free response when asked What is your biggest struggle when using GroupMe. A majority of users expressed that the app does not properly display notifications to new messages. When users open the app when alerted of a new message, the group chat or direct message will take a long time to load, leaving users to search through chats for the new message. One respondent states that The group me app will say I have a notification however when I open the app to see what the notification is, it goes away temporarily. Another struggle expressed by respondents was the lack of differentiating groups. The layout of the chat page is a little too basic, having a lot of groups is hard to navigate, especially when a lot of groups have the same core people in them.

57.1% of survey respondents answered that the Chat function on GroupMe Can be a lot of information at once but is bearable to use. 14.3% of respondents felt that the Chat function was Very disorganized and difficult to navigate. These responses reveal that a majority of users have some difficulties with the amount of information presented in GroupMes Chat function.

Survey respondents were given options of potential features they would enjoy/appreciate when using GroupMe. More than half of the survey respondents selected Pinning Direct Messages/Group Chats and Categorizing Direct Messages/Group Chats as features that would elevate their user experience.

Design Problem Statement

Pain Point #1: New Notifications

Survey respondents expressed that GroupMe does not properly display new messages after receiving notifications for them outside of the app. When the app takes time to update and reveal which direct message or group chat have new messages, users are left searching multiple different chats to find the new message.

Paint Point #2: Navigating Multiple Group Chats

As articulated in the research responses, users experience difficulties in differentiating the different messages that they receive from direct messages and group chats. Some group chats may hold more importance than others, but without a means to separate or categorize them, users are left treating all messages as the same importance.

Design Question: How might we improve the experience of users navigating the chat feature in the GroupMe App?

The Solution

Ideation

After reflecting on the survey responses, I felt that it was most important to create a quick and easy access to the new messages that a user has received. These new messages would be found in a tab located towards the top of the Chat function to make it easily accessible to the users. Beneath the New Messages tab would be an area for folders customized by the user. The folder would be named after the area of messages, ie. Classes -> class specific Group Chats. Within each folder would be a list of Direct Messages/ Group Chats that relate to the folder it is found within.

User Flows

Wireframes

After establishing the flow of the new design, I create wireframes to organize the New Messages and Custom Folder tabs. I followed the previous design of GroupMe’s chat function but made New Messages the most prominent tab on the page.

Mockups

The mockups follow the same design layout of GroupMe, utilizing the blue brand colors for the New Message and Custom Folders tab icons.

This design implementation prevents the difficulties of scrolling in search of an unread message or of finding a specific group chat. As such a viable option for communication this edits can keep the app organized and easy to use for the average college student or professor.
